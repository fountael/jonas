Jonas Salk

American medical researcher and virologist who discovered and developed one of the first successful polio vaccines.

Timeline



  • 1914 Born in New York City.

  • 1939 Became a scientist physician at Mount Sinai Hospital.

  • 1942 Went to the University of Michigan on a research fellowship to develop an influenza vaccine.

  • 1947 Was appointed director of the Virus Research Laboratory at the University of Pittsburgh School of Medicine.

  • 1954 National testing of Salk's vaccine began on one million children, ages six to nine, who became known as the Polio Pioneers.

  • 1955 That vaccine was announced safe and effective.

  • 1962 Polio cases dropped from 45,000 to 910.

  • 1963 Salk Institute for Biological Studies in La Jolla was founded.

  • 1995 Passed away at the age of 80
