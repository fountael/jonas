Jonas Salk

“There is hope in dreams, imagination, and in the courage of those who wish to make those dreams a reality.”

Learn More

  • 1914 Salk was born in New York City.
  • 1939 Salk became a scientist physician at Mount Sinai Hospital.
  • 1942 Salk went to the University of Michigan on a research fellowship to develop an influenza vaccine.
  • 1947 Salk was appointed director of the Virus Research Laboratory at the University of Pittsburgh School of Medicine.
  • 1954 National testing of Salk's vaccine began on one million children, ages six to nine, who became known as the Polio Pioneers.
  • 1955 That vaccine was announced safe and effective.
  • 1962 Polio cases dropped from 45,000 to 910.
  • 1963 Salk Institute for Biological Studies in La Jolla was founded.
  • 1995 Salk passed away at the age of 80
